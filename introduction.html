Intro to Password Managers

Now is the time to change your behavior, and learn about the password manager

Issues of Passwords

The number of passwords that you need to create might have significantly increased due to the increasing number of applications and websites you need to sign up for. The safest way to store your password is just to memorize them all. However, you know that most of us are not able to memorize all our passwords except we reuse one or two for all of our accounts
If you are using the same weak password for your accounts, then you leave yourself open to password-based attacks.

Definition & Example

Definition Example
Password managers are here to secure your digital life. A password manager is a program that allows you to generate complex passwords and store all your passwords fully encrypted in a safe location. Generated passwords by the password manager make your online world less vulnerable to password-based attacks. Browser-based password managers like Google Chrome, Firefox, and Safari are the most common type of password manager and it stores your passwords in the cloud. Storing in a cloud means your passwords are always accessible for you from anywhere at any time. Your passwords in browser-based password managers are encrypted before being stored in the cloud servers

What are the benefits of using a password manager?

Using a password manager can greatly increase your accounts by essentially enforcing security best practices. Here are some of the important advantages.

Autosave & fill

You don't have to remember complex passwords

Protection

It protects your information from cyber security threats

Browser-based
